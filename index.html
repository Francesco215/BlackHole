Some consequences of Black Hole Thermodynamics

The maximum entropy a region of space can cointain is proportional to the surface area, this would lead to a paradox if the region of space is infinite, here we explore the hypotesis and some consequences

Why a universe can't be flat and infinite

The entropy of a black hole is \frac{k_bc^3}{4G\hbar}A=\alpha V^{2/3} where \alpha=\frac{k_bc^3}{4G\hbar}\sqrt[3]{\pi}\bigg(\frac34\bigg) and $A$ and $V$ are the area and the volume of the black hole, Therefore the entropy per unit volume of a black hole is s_b=\frac SV=\frac\alpha{\sqrt[3]{V}} And it decreases as the volume of the black hole gets larger and larger.

The main property of black holes that we are going to keep in mind is that a black hole of volume V has the maximum entropy any spherical volume V can have. Suppose we have a universe enclosed in a volume of radius R and that on large scales we can say that it has constant entropy density $s_u=\frac{dS}{dV}$. Therefore the maximum volume $V_{max}$ of this universe is. V_{max}=\bigg(\frac \alpha{s_u}\bigg)^3 Therefore the univere can’t be infinite if it is homogeneus on large scales and flat

Why it expands

Suppose the volume of the Universe for some reason is exactly $V_{max}$ it would last a blink of an eye before collapsing into a black hole. That is because the total entropy has to increase. If the universe has some kind of law that keeps it’s volume always equal to $V_{max}$ we would have an expanding universe. This could explain why our universe in expanding, and why the expantion of the universe isn’t constant throught the age of the universe.

Some remarks

If the universe can be described by a single wavefunction $|\textrm{Universe}\rangle$, than by definition the total entropy of the universe will be zero, that being said, if you consider finite region of space It can be said equivalently that you don't know anything about the universe is outside a region of space, this makes the entropy for a limited region of space non zero since you do the partial trace over everything that is outside that volume , than the entropy can be considered non zero and the paradox reappears for a large enough volume
